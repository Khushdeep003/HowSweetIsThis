About Dairy Milk Chocolate

Dairy milk chocolate is a type of milk chocolate that is made with milk powder or condensed milk along with cocoa solids and sugar. It was first developed by the British chocolate company Cadbury in 1905, and it quickly became one of their most popular products. Dairy milk chocolate has a creamy, smooth texture and a sweet, chocolatey flavor that many people find very appealing.

History

Dairy Milk was first launched by Cadbury in 1905. It was one of the first chocolates to be sold in a bar rather than in boxes or tins. Over the years, the recipe has been tweaked, and today's Dairy Milk contains a higher proportion of milk than the original recipe.
